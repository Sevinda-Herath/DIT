1. Participation Rules

Equipment

  • For PC games, players may bring their own peripherals (keyboard, mouse, mousepad) with long enough cables and any necessary adapters.
  • PS2 keyboards will not be supported.
  • For mobile games, players are expected to bring their own phones and peripherals (headsets, battery packs, etc.).
  • For console games, players must bring their own headphones.
  • Audio equipment will not be provided; all players are required to bring their own headphones with long enough cables.
  • Team names will not be changed after registrations are closed and the draw is published.

Names – Team Names, Player Aliases

A team or player’s name, icon, profile picture or logo may not contain any of the following:

  • Profanities
  • Obscene or vulgar language
  • Racist, anti-Semitic or inciting hatred
  • Gang affiliation
  • Drugs
  • Sexual material
  • Offensive material

Using alternative spelling, gibberish, or wrong spelling to avoid these requirements is forbidden. Players or teams will not be allowed to take part.

Account Sharing

  • Account sharing in online tournaments across all games is strictly prohibited. All participants must have their own accounts.
  • If found, this will lead to disqualification of the team and a ban on the offending player(s) in future tournaments.

Multi‑clanning or Multi‑team Participation

  • No player is allowed to be part of multiple teams or gaming clans.
  • Players found to be multi‑clanning or in multiple teams will be banned for a minimum period of one year.
  • All teams that the player was a part of will be disqualified.

Player Accounts

  • Players can only use the accounts they have registered with for the respective tournament.
  • Playing under another player’s account, or soliciting/inducing someone else to do so, is prohibited.
  • During online tournaments, players cannot change their submitted account details after they have played their first match (unless overruled by game‑specific rules).

2. Game Rules

Pre‑Match Ready‑up (Warm‑up) Time (LAN tournaments)

  • Pre‑match ready‑up time is a maximum of 10 minutes to plug in devices and install drivers (ask for permission first).
  • Team coaches are allowed in the match area during the warm‑up period only.
  • If a coach is found communicating with the team during tournament play, the team may be disqualified at the discretion of Nebula Esports Staff and penalties imposed.

Removable USB Storage and Media Devices (LAN tournaments)

  • No USB storage devices should be plugged into any tournament PCs under any circumstance. Failure to comply may lead to penalties.

Tournament Formats and Match Sets

  • Tournament formats (double‑elimination or knockout) will be announced on the tournament page on Nebula Esports.
  • The number of match sets during a tournament (best of 3 or 5) depends on the game title.
  • In double‑elimination tournaments, finals should be a best of 5 games (exceptions at the discretion of Nebula Esports).
  • In double‑elimination best‑of‑5 finals, upper‑bracket finalists will receive an advantage.
  • In double‑elimination best‑of‑3 finals, upper‑bracket finalists will not receive an advantage.
  • At LAN tournaments, due to unavoidable circumstances, Nebula Esports staff may change match sets and will inform on the fly at the event.
  • Unless specified, coaches cannot be in the match area while a game is being played (including pauses, half‑time, etc.).
  • Dota 2: One coach is allowed in the match area during the draft phase only and must leave immediately after the draft.
  • CS:GO: One coach may communicate only during warmup, half‑time, or during one of the timeouts the teams can call.

Responsibility

  • Players and participants cannot refuse to have their matches broadcast by Nebula Esports or any authorized broadcaster, nor choose how the match is broadcast.

Tournament Stream Delay

  • Tournament streams will be delayed (approximately 2–5 minutes) to avoid player sniping. Abusing a tournament stream will result in immediate disqualification and severe penalties.

3. Player Conduct

Match Area (LAN tournaments)

  • The match area is the area immediately surrounding competition PCs used during tournament play. Tournament Staff may define the match area at their discretion.
  • After a match commences, only participating players, broadcasters and organizers are allowed in the area.
  • No managers, well‑wishers, coaches, etc. can remain in the playing area once matches begin (unless specifically allowed).
  • Players should not leave the match area during pauses between games without permission. Remain seated at the terminal until the match is complete.
  • No eating or smoking in the match area or near PCs. Smoking/vaping is strictly forbidden in the match area.
  • Beverages are allowed only below the table. Tables must be kept clear and clean.
  • No electronic devices, cameras or similar devices in the gaming area unless prior permission is given. Such devices must be handed to officials before setup.
  • All mobile phones must be switched off and not used during a match (including during pause time, half‑times, etc.).
  • Exaggerated loud noise and offensive language are forbidden.
  • Paper documents for tactical purposes are allowed in reasonable sizes/numbers. Electronic versions (even on tournament PCs) are strictly forbidden.

Tournament PC Setup (LAN tournaments)

  • All tournament PCs will have TeamSpeak and/or Discord installed. A local TeamSpeak server will be provided.
  • Any attempt to overclock or alter default PC configuration for performance is strictly forbidden and can lead to immediate loss or disqualification.
  • Any attempt to download/install additional software or media on any match PC is strictly forbidden.
  • Using the Internet on tournament PCs for anything other than gameplay is forbidden (e.g., streaming music/videos).
  • Teams who require configs/drivers saved on tournament PCs need to upload via the Nebula Esports web page.

Walkovers

  • A team may award a walkover if unable to play or have the full team present only during qualifier rounds or group stage matches.
  • Any team that reaches semifinals or finals and decides to give a walkover will be disqualified; the next best team will be allowed to play (or as decided by staff).
  • If two teams are deemed suitable, the choice will be by coin toss.
  • Disqualified teams will not receive any prize money and will be suspended for at least one upcoming Nebula Esports Ranked Tournament; this suspension extends to the players.
  • Nebula Esports staff may override these rules at their discretion depending on the situation.

Indecent Behaviour

  • Swearing or any hostility, racist or any abuse (verbal or physical) towards another player is strictly prohibited (including in‑game chat and verbal exchanges).
  • Insults on IRC, IM programs, social media, email or other means will be punished if evidence is clear and presented to Tournament Staff.
  • Severe abuse with radical statements or threats of physical violence can lead to heavier penalties including disqualification from current and/or future tournaments.
  • At LAN tournaments, mind your language during matches.
  • At LAN tournaments, players are strictly prohibited from being under the influence of alcohol or psychoactive drugs in the match area. Smoking, vaping, drinking alcohol or using any psychoactive drugs in the match area is strictly prohibited.

Insults, Racial Slurs or Harassment

  • Zero tolerance policy for any harassment, including demeaning language, threats, hate crimes, intimidation, non‑consensual photography/recording, stalking, inappropriate physical contact, unwelcome physical attention, or statements expressing contempt of ethnicity, religion, sexual orientation, citizenship, appearance, body size, age, disability, or gender identity.
  • Depending on severity, penalties may include disqualification from current and/or future tournaments.
  • Every participant must show respect towards other participants, organizers, staff and casters. This applies to everyone at both LAN and online events.

Physical Assaults (LAN tournaments)

  • Any participant who attacks or physically harms another participant, player, staff, attendee, press, sponsor or guest will be disqualified and banned from all future tournaments for a period of 2 years.
  • The team which the participant represents will be disqualified from the tournament and banned from Nebula Esports Ranked Tournaments for 6 months.
  • Depending on severity, legal charges may be pursued.

Compete in a Professional Manner

  • Throwing a match, halting play without cause, or flagrant lack of effort will be penalized.

Property Damage & Theft (LAN tournaments)

  • Intentional or accidental damages to hardware/property must be paid by the person(s) responsible.
  • Hardware changes (e.g., unplugging peripherals without consent) or software changes to PCs will be considered damage to property.
  • Intentional or accidental damage/soiling of venue space, furniture, equipment will be considered damage to property.
  • Alcohol and drugs are not allowed in match areas.
  • People found intoxicated or under the influence may be denied entry to the match area/venue and shown off the premises.
  • Smoking is strictly forbidden indoors and allowed only in designated outdoor areas. Electronic cigarettes are also not allowed indoors.

Report Any Harassment or Abuse (LAN tournaments)

  • Report any harassment immediately to a Nebula Esports Staff member (clearly tagged as STAFF).

Limitations (LAN tournaments)

  • Player conduct restrictions apply upon entry from the gate of the tournament premises. Incidents beyond the premises or during transit are not covered by these rules.

Cheating of Any Kind

  • Cheating (in‑game cheats, faking results/demos, ringing, playing with a disallowed player, impersonation, etc.) will face severe penalties including lifetime bans. Game‑specific rules may add further detail.

Competition Manipulation

  • Offering money/benefits, making threats, or exerting pressure to influence match results is prohibited and may result in lifetime bans for players and maximum penalties for team officials/staff/management.

Impersonation

  • Impersonating any player, staff member, referee, sponsor or partner will result in severe penalties including bans.

Disruptions Due to Players

  • Any verbal/physical abuse to another participant, player, staff, attendee, press, sponsor or guest will result in disqualification and bans from future tournaments. Other disruptions will be handled as appropriate.

4. Penalties for Breach

General Player Penalties

  • Warning: Accumulating three warnings results in disqualification.
  • Game Loss: The team loses the current game regardless of the result; a win is awarded to the opponent.
  • Disqualification: The team is ejected from the tournament and cannot proceed.
  • No Show: Teams that register and don’t show up will be marked as no‑show; ranking points reduced and the scheduled match becomes a walkover.
  • Ban: In extreme cases, bans can apply to the ongoing and future tournaments.

Penalties on Player Conduct

Swearing/hostility towards opponents or staff/spectators:

  • First infraction: Warning and suitable penalty (e.g., draft penalty in Dota 2).
  • Second infraction: Forfeiture of a ban.
  • Third infraction: Forfeiture of the match.

Penalties on Unprofessional Behavior

Throwing a match, halting play without cause, or lack of effort:

  • First infraction: Warning and suitable penalty (e.g., draft penalty).
  • Second infraction: Forfeiture of a ban.
  • Third infraction: Forfeiture of the match.

5. Procedure

Based on the violation, the following line of communication will be used to inform of any penalty:

  • First: Inform the Player(s) in question
  • Second: Inform the Team Captain
  • Last: Inform the Clan Leader (if applicable)

If the Player or Team Captain wishes to dispute or challenge the decision, they may follow the steps set out in the dispute clause.

6. Event Security

  • During LAN events, players, spectators and visitors may be searched or scanned by security personnel before admission to the venue.
  • If banned objects are found, security may confiscate the object and refuse admission.
  • Access control will prevent weapons or hazardous substances at the event.
  • Items banned across all events include (non‑exhaustive): drugs; projectiles/explosives; flammable products/spray cans; pyrotechnics; weapons/sharp objects; discriminating/provocative banners or items; animals; drones; any other objects (umbrellas, camera tripods, laser pens, etc.) deemed risky by security. The list may change depending on venue restrictions.

7. Rule Changes

The Organizer reserves the right to ignore or overrule any rules to make the competition as fair as possible. Teams and participants acknowledge the Organizer’s right to modify rules and regulations as needed (e.g., on game patch releases). Such modifications will be publicly announced and participants informed.
